Introduction

Is GDP a good measure of happiness?

The topic of whether a country's happiness is solely determined by its GDP is a subject of ongoing debate. While some argue that GDP is the sole determinant of a country's happiness, others hold the opinion that there are multifarious factors that contribute to a nation's well-being. For this assignment, I have chosen to examine the dataset of the World Happiness Report, which sheds light on this contentious issue.

I have taken the data from the following website: Kaggle - World Happiness Report

Left Graph
  • The graph indicates a consistent decline in happiness levels across countries, irrespective of their GDP values.
  • The x-axis represents several countries
  • Along the y-axis, the graph shows two lines, one for Happiness Score (shown in blue) and one for GDP (shown in orange).

This intriguing observation shows that variables other than GDP may be impacting the drop in happiness levels.

Right Graph
  • The graph shows a clear positive correlation, indicating that countries with higher GDP tend to have higher happiness levels.
  • x-axis represents Happiness Score.
  • y-axis represents GDP values for various countries.

This suggests that countries with superior economic performance, as measured by GDP, have higher levels of happiness.

Personal Opinion

Aside from GDP, a variety of other factors influence happiness. Environmental quality, health and education levels, technological improvements, and other factors can all influence a country's happiness rating. Happiness is a complex phenomena that economic statistics alone cannot fully convey.
